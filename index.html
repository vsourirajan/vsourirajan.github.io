Relative Positional Encoding (RPE) for Vision Transformers | Feb 2024 - May 2024

We examine 3 parameterizations (general learnable function, monotonically decreasing function, ratio of two polynomial functions) of reltive positional encodings for vision transformers, leveraging the Euclidean distance between centers of patches as the distance mechanism for the RPE functions. We also implement two subvariants (same RPE parameters across all attention heads, different RPE parameters for different attention heads). We evaluated each implementation on MNIST and CIFAR10 datasets and achieved up to 0.15 increase in test accuracy in comparison to RPE-free baseline.

Optimal Toll Pricing for NYC Bridges and Tunnels | Jan 2024 - May 2024

We formulate traffic congestion as a continuous-time Markov Chain (CTMC) stochastic process, fitting model parameters using 10 years of hourly traffic data and constrained maximum likelihood estimation. We develop a pricing schema from the steady state distribution of the CTMC, which dynamically adjusts toll pricing according to the congestion state. We achieved a simulated 50% reduction in congestion during peak hours from a $3.50 toll price increase.

Acoustic and Language Analysis for Suicidal Ideation among U.S. Veterans (BioDataMining 2021) | Jun 2019 - Feb 2021

We develop a classifier based on speech markers to screen for suicidal ideation in US Veterans. Veterans submitted ~600 narrative audio recordings and we extracted acoustic features from the audios and linguistic features from the transcribed text. We applied several classical machine learning models, experimenting with combinations of acoustic and semantics features, achieving an AUC of 0.80. This was the first study to apply machine learning to detect suicidal ideation among U.S. Veterans from speech data collected in a real-life setting.
